# Bloom Again, Nemo

# User Experience Roadmap

> This document describes the complete emotional journey experienced by the visitor.
>
> Unlike ROADMAP.md, which describes technical implementation, this document describes the experience from the visitor's perspective.
>
> Every feature implemented in the application should support one or more stages defined here.
>
> If a technical implementation does not improve the experience described in this document, its necessity should be questioned.

---

# Purpose

Bloom Again, Nemo is not designed around screens.

It is designed around emotions.

The visitor should never feel like they are navigating an application.

Instead, they should feel like they are participating in a carefully crafted story.

Every interaction exists for emotional reasons before technical ones.

# Stage 1

Arrival

Goal

Create immediate curiosity.

The visitor scans the QR code.

The application opens instantly.

There should be no forms.

No loading spinners.

No navigation menus.

No unnecessary information.

Only a peaceful scene.

Success Criteria

The visitor immediately feels curious.

---

# Stage 2

Observation

Goal

Allow the visitor to absorb the environment.

Flowers gently move.

Soft particles float.

The night background feels calm.

Nothing asks the visitor to interact yet.

The application patiently waits.

Emotion

Calmness.

Safety.

Curiosity.

---

# Stage 3

Bloom

Goal

The world slowly comes alive.

Flowers bloom.

Grass grows.

Lights appear.

The scene becomes magical.

The visitor realizes something is about to happen.

Emotion

Wonder.

Anticipation.

---

# Stage 4

Silence

Goal

Pause.

Nothing important should happen immediately after the bloom.

The pause creates expectation.

The visitor naturally begins searching the screen.

This silence is intentional.

It should never feel like loading.

Emotion

Expectation.

---

# Stage 5

Envelope Entrance

Goal

Introduce the central object.

The envelope enters the scene.

It should feel physical.

It should feel slightly magical.

The movement should never appear robotic.

The envelope gently settles among the flowers.

Emotion

Discovery.

---

# Stage 6

Idle

Goal

Invite interaction.

Nothing flashes.

Nothing bounces.

Nothing demands attention.

The envelope simply waits.

Small floating motion communicates that it can be interacted with.

Emotion

Invitation.

---

# Stage 7

Opening

Goal

Reward curiosity.

The visitor taps the envelope.

The flap slowly opens.

The paper begins to emerge.

Nothing happens instantly.

The visitor should feel as though they are opening a real letter.

Emotion

Excitement.

---

# Stage 8

Letter Reveal

Goal

Reveal the emotional content.

The paper slides upward.

The envelope becomes secondary.

The letter becomes the visual focus.

The transition should feel elegant.

Emotion

Connection.

---

# Stage 9

Reading

Goal

Allow uninterrupted reading.

Animations become subtle.

Nothing distracts from the words.

The visitor's attention belongs entirely to the message.

Emotion

Reflection.

Warmth.

Affection.

---

# Stage 10

Participation

Goal

Invite the visitor to respond.

The application gently asks for a photo.

This should never feel like filling out a form.

Instead, it should feel like replying to a handwritten letter.

Emotion

Reciprocity.

---

# Stage 11

Upload

Goal

Celebrate participation.

Selecting a photo should feel rewarding.

Uploading should feel safe.

Progress should be reassuring rather than technical.

Emotion

Contribution.

---

# Stage 12

Reward

Goal

Thank the visitor.

The application surprises them with a small reward.

Examples include:

Special flower petals.

Butterflies.

Hidden quotes.

Special stationery.

Golden seals.

Rewards should always feel like gifts.

Never achievements.

Emotion

Gratitude.

---

# Stage 13

Goodbye

Goal

End peacefully.

The visitor should leave with a positive emotional memory.

The application should never end abruptly.

The final screen should quietly encourage returning.

Emotion

Closure.

---

# Stage 14

Return Visit

Goal

Recognize loyalty.

Returning visitors should not receive exactly the same experience.

The application should acknowledge that they came back.

Examples

Different greetings.

Different flowers.

Seasonal decorations.

Special rewards.

New stationery.

The experience should evolve over time.

Emotion

Belonging.

---

---

# Design Rule

Every future feature should answer one question:

Which stage of the visitor journey does this improve?

If the answer is:

"None."

The feature probably does not belong in the project.

---

# Final Statement

Bloom Again, Nemo is remembered not because of its code, its animations, or its technology.

It is remembered because of how it makes someone feel.

This document exists to ensure that every future decision preserves that experience.
